Three-dimensional mapping and localization in dynamic large-scale environment

Advisor: Howie Choset | Robotics Institute | Carnegie Mellon University | Jul 2018 - Present

Three-dimensional mapping and localization in dynamic and large-scale environments is still a big challenge for mobile robots. On one hand, much previous work did not consider the effect of dynamic obstacles, and they did not perform well in the dynamic environment. On the other hand, changes in viewpoints can also cause many rotation-sensitive algorithms to wrongly match features of the same place but different visual features. In this work, we are focusing on these two challenges and trying to filter dynamic obstacles and learn rotation-invariant features to achieve SLAM in a large-scale dynamic environment.

Clustering and anamoly detection of log templates in IT systems

Advisor: Dan Pei | Department of Computer Science | Tsinghua University | Jan 2018 - Apr 2019

Logs provide detailed runtime information of IT systems and allow operators to monitor the machines and troubleshoot the anomalous situations in advance. The unstructured nature of log data makes it a huge challenge to accurately parse raw log data into structured templates. We found that previous algorithms of extracting log templates can not perform well in some cases. Therefore, we propose an algorithm of clustering templates to post-process the results and improve the performance of templates extraction. Also, in order to detect the anomalous situation from all log data, we utilize an algorithm of change-point detection based on Bayesian optimization. The evaluation result of logs in the real-world shows the availability of this Bayesian change-point algorithm.

Precursor prediction of alert events of IT systems

Advisor: Dan Pei | Department of Computer Science | Tsinghua University | Nov 2018 - Jan 2019

The tremendous alert events of IT systems act as important resources to monitor the running states of machines and provide a lot of precursors for system failures. However, mining these precursors from previous data and online forecasting the probability of system failures has been a challenge for IT operators. We utilized the algorithm of frequent patterns mining to find frequent patterns of system failures and predict the system failures according to their supporting probability. Also, we tried to predict the imminent system failure from the specific alert pattern and utilized a hidden semi-Markov model to achieve online system failure prediction.

Face recognition and verification based on UniformFace

Advisor: Jiwen Lu | Department of Automation | Tsinghua University | Oct 2017 - Mar 2018

With the advanced network architectures and the discriminative learning approaches, deep CNNs have boosted the face recognition(FR) performance to unprecedented levels. In previous work, an A-Softmax loss for CNNs was proposed to learning discriminative face features with clear geometric interpretation, which intrinsically matches the prior that faces lie on a spherical manifold. Inspired by the idea of center loss, we were interested in assigning constraints to different classes of features, making them as evenly distributed as possible in the spherical feature space. The evaluation results on YTF, LFW and Megaface datasets show the better performance of our method --- UniformFace.

Gesture recognition based on channel state information(CSI)

Advisor: Yuan He | Department of Software | Tsinghua University | Jul 2017 - Sep 2017

In wireless communications, channel state information(CSI) describes how a signal propagates from the transmitter to the receiver and represents the combined effect. Therefore, we were interested in utilizing this signal to detect and recognize specific patterns. In this project, we utilized a feature extraction method based on Principal Component Analysis and Discrete Wavelet Transform to extract discriminate features from the 30-dimensional WIFI signal, and then trained an SVM classifier to recognize the specific 9 gestures. The result shows the availability of our method.
