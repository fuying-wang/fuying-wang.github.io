Research experience

Three-dimensional mapping and localization in dynamic large-scale environment

Advisor: Howie Choset | Robotics Institute | Carnegie Mellon University | Jul 2018 - Present

Three-dimensional mapping and localization in dynamic and large-scale environments is still a big challenge for mobile robots. On one hand, much previous work did not consider the effect of dynamic obstacles, and they did not perform well in the dynamic environment. On the other hand, changes in viewpoints can also cause many rotation-sensitive algorithms to wrongly match features of the same place but different visual features. In this work, we are focusing on these two challenges and trying to filter dynamic obstacles and learn rotation-invariant features to achieve SLAM in a large-scale dynamic environment.

Clustering and anamoly detection of log templates in IT systems

Advisor: Dan Pei | Department of Computer Science | Tsinghua University | Jan 2018 - Apr 2019

Logs provide detailed runtime information of IT systems and allow operators to monitor the machines and troubleshoot the anomalous situations in advance. The unstructured nature of log data makes it a huge challenge to accurately parse raw log data into structured templates. We found that previous algorithms of extracting log templates can not perform well in some cases. Therefore, we propose an algorithm of clustering templates to post-process the results and improve the performance of templates extraction. Besides, in order to detect the anomalous situation from all log data, we utilize an algorithm of change-point detection based on Bayesian optimization. The evaluation result of logs in the real-world shows the availability of this Bayesian change-point algorithm.
